## 😤 PROBLEM SECTION

**Section Header:**
Sound familiar?

**Body copy:**

You buy books faster than you finish them. The stack on your nightstand isn't a reading list — it's a guilt trip.

You've tried everything. Goodreads, where reviews go to collect dust. Discord book clubs that go quiet by week three because the conversation has nothing to pull people back. IRL book clubs that collapse the moment someone can't make it. Group chats that spiral into everything except the chapter anyone actually read.

The problem isn't you. It's that reading has always been something you do alone and report on later — and "later" never quite arrives.

The last time you finished a book regularly, you weren't alone. You were in a class, or a club, or just happened to have a friend reading the same thing. Someone was alongside you. That made all the difference.

That's what's been missing. Not another app to log books. A place to actually read with someone.

## ✅ SOLUTION SECTION

**Section Header:**
This is Flipbook.

**Body copy:**

Flipbook is a social reading app built for the people who keep meaning to finish the book. You create a club like you'd create a WhatsApp group — invite your friends, pick a book, start reading. As you read inside the app, your club members' reactions and comments appear right in the margins of the page you're both on.

Not in a separate chat. Not after you're done. On the page. In real time.

When you hit the paragraph that breaks your heart and someone in your club already left a reaction on it, reading alone stops being possible. That's the moment Flipbook was built for.

**Feature callouts:**

**Clubs in seconds**
Make a club the same way you'd start a group chat. Share one link. Everyone's in. No scheduling, no hosting, no snacks required.

**Talk on the page**
Reactions and comments live right next to the paragraph that prompted them. No spoilers ahead. No dead forum threads after the fact. The conversation is exactly where you are in the book.

**See who's reading alongside you**
A soft, gentle view of where your club is in the book. Close enough to feel together, not close enough to make you feel behind.

**Follow the writers you love**
Indie authors and serialized writers publish on Flipbook and turn their readers into communities. When they drop a new chapter, you'll know — and the room opens.

**Read offline, anywhere**
Open a book in Flipbook. Read on your commute. We'll catch up when you do.

## ❓ FAQ SECTION

**Q: What is Flipbook, exactly?**
A: A social reading app for mobile. You read books inside the app, and your club members' reactions appear in the margins of the page in real time. Think of it as the book club that's always in session — no scheduling, no dead Discord servers, no guilt.

**Q: When does beta open?**
A: We're targeting a closed beta shortly. If you're on the waitlist, you'll get an invite code before anyone else. We'll email you the moment it's ready.

**Q: How does the waitlist work?**
A: Drop your email below. We'll store it, keep you updated on what we're building, and send you an invite code to sign up on the real app when beta opens. No spam. One email when it's your turn.

**Q: Is it free?**
A: The core experience is free. We'll have a Pro tier for power readers who want more clubs, more offline reading, and advanced customization — but the magic moment? That's free for everyone.

**Q: Will I need to upload my own books?**
A: Club moderators can upload PDFs privately to their club. Think of it like sharing a file in a group chat — private to your club, not searchable, not redistributable.

**Q: I've tried Goodreads, Fable, Discord book clubs. Why is this different?**
A: Fair question. Goodreads is for the books you've finished. Discord book clubs die because the conversation isn't tied to the book. Flipbook fixes the primitive — the reactions live on the page itself, so the conversation only happens where you actually are in the book. It's a different thing, not a prettier version of the same thing.

**Q: I mostly read alone. Is Flipbook for me?**
A: You still can. Solo reading works fine. The clubs are there when you want them — and most readers find they want them more than they expected.

**Q: Is it iOS only?**
A: iOS and Android at launch.

## 💡 OPTIMIZATION NOTES

**A/B Test Ideas:**
- Headline A: "Read with the people who are reading right now." vs. Headline B: "The book club that's always in session."
- CTA A: "Join the waitlist" vs. CTA B: "Reserve my spot" vs. CTA C: "Get early access"
- Hero with tagline "Read together. Finish more. Talk on the page." vs. current subheadline

**Conversion Tips:**
- The qualifier field ("What's the last book you finished?") should be positioned as optional — it gives you user research and great launch content without adding friction.
- The FAQ objection about "I've tried [competitors]" is critical — it should render early in the FAQ and be specific. Readers who've been burned before need proof this is different, not just different-sounding.
- On mobile, the problem section should come before the first CTA — mobile visitors convert better when they've felt the pain point before they're asked to act.
- Consider seeding the hero with a one-line quote from a beta reader once you have one. Social proof at the hero level significantly outperforms social proof buried mid-page.
- "Be among the first to read when beta opens" performs better than urgency language like "limited spots" — Flipbook's brand doesn't do artificial scarcity.
- The "How it works" section serves double duty: it answers "what happens after I sign up?" (waitlist-specific) and "what does the app actually do?" (product-specific). Keep both answers visible.

**Brand Reminders:**
- Never use the word "platform" in user-facing copy.
- No exclamation points in notifications or CTAs.
- No "journey," "reimagined," "ecosystem," or "leverage."
- The tone is: a friend who runs book clubs tells you about an app she's been testing. Not a press release. Not a pitch deck.
